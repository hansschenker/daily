In computer science, 
a dispatcher typically denotes a specific software module 
that manages the allocation of CPU control to a chosen process or thread. 

This idea is mainly found in operating systems, where it plays a vital role in the process/thread management system.

Conceptual Mapping
In classical operating systems, a dispatcher is the mechanism responsible for:

- Selecting the next unit of execution (process or thread) after the scheduler has made a     
  policy decision.
- Performing the actual context switch: saving the current execution context and restoring the 
  next one.
- Determining when and how the next piece of work begins execution.

RxJS schedulers fulfill a closely parallel role within the observable pipeline:

They control the execution context and timing of three primary actions:

- Subscription (when the observable begins work, via subscribeOn).
- Notification delivery (when values, errors, or completion are delivered to observers, via 
  observeOn).
- Scheduling of delayed or periodic operations (used internally by operators such as delay, 
  sinterval, timer, observeOn, etc.).


By centralizing control over these timing and threading decisions in a single abstraction
the Scheduler in RxJS effectively makes it 
- the central point 
      - through which all asynchronous or deferred work is dispatched. 

This mirrors the dispatcher's responsibility in an OS kernel for routing execution to the appropriate context.
